Bio

Christopher is a Humanities teacher at Town School for Boys in San Francisco, California. After a fellowship at Rye Country Day School, a M.A in English Education at Teachers College of Columbia, and 3 years at George Jackson Academy in NYC, Christopher made his way west to San Francisco. He has a deep interest in engaging students, and helping them to see the connections between their learning and their own lives. In the classroom, Chris is most often engaged in Inquiry, Arts, and Project-Based Learning, as well as elements of Gamification.

Religion aside, education is the single biggest influence on each one of our lives. We know that education neither starts nor ends in the classroom, but we do certainly clock a considerable chunk of time inside these walls. The question for me as a teacher is, “How can we, inside the classroom, equip students to take control of the education they will give and receive in the world outside?”
